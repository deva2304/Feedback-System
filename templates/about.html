About the Model

Understanding the Correlation-Constrained Latent Factor approach to implicit feedback recommendation.

📄 Research Title

Correlation-Constrained Latent Factor Models for Implicit Feedback Recommendation Systems

This project implements a novel approach to ad recommendation that combines matrix factorization of user-item implicit interactions with correlation-constrained regularization of the latent factor space. Unlike explicit feedback systems (star ratings), our model learns from implicit behavioral signals — clicks, view time, scroll depth, cart additions, and purchase events — using confidence-weighted factorization inspired by Hu, Koren & Volinsky (2008).

🧬 How It Works — The CCLF Pipeline

Our system follows a rigorous multi-stage pipeline:

  • Step 1 — Implicit Feedback Collection: We collect behavioral signals from data.csv: click counts, view time, scroll depth, add-to-cart events, purchase events, search queries, and session duration. These are implicit signals — the user never explicitly rates anything.
  • Step 2 — Confidence-Weighted Interaction Matrix: For each user-item pair, we compute a weighted implicit signal: rui = ∑ wk · xk, then derive a confidence level cui = 1 + α · rui (with α={{ model_meta.alpha_confidence }}). Higher confidence means the system is more certain about the user's preference.
  • Step 3 — Truncated SVD Factorization: We factorize the confidence-weighted user-item matrix ({{ model_meta.n_users }} × {{ model_meta.n_items }}) via truncated SVD to extract k={{ model_meta.k_latent }} latent factors, capturing {{ model_meta.explained_variance }}% of total variance. This gives us user factors P and item factors Q.
  • Step 4 — Correlation Constraint: We compute the Pearson correlation matrix across all 8 behavioral features, then extract its top eigenvectors via SVD. Item factors are adjusted: Qadj = (1-λ)Q + λ · corr_projection, constraining representations to respect behavioral correlations (e.g., the strong add_to_cart ↔ purchase correlation of r=0.57).
  • Step 5 — Recommendation Scoring: For each user-product pair, we compute score = Pu · Qadj,i — a dot product in the correlation-constrained latent space. Products are ranked by this score.

📐 Mathematical Formulation

The core mathematical framework of the CCLF model:

rui = ∑k wk · xui,k — Weighted Implicit Signal
cui = 1 + α · rui — Confidence Level (α={{ model_meta.alpha_confidence }})
R̃ = √C ⊙ R ≈ UΣVT — Truncated SVD (k={{ model_meta.k_latent }})
Ω = XTX / (n-1),   Ω = UΩΣΩVΩT — Correlation Basis
Qadj = (1-λ)Q̂ + λ · (behavioral · UΩ[:k]) — Correlation Constraint
score(u, i) = Pu · Qadj,iT — Latent Space Dot Product

Where λ={{ model_meta.lambda_corr }} controls the strength of the correlation constraint. Higher λ forces item representations to align more strongly with the behavioral correlation structure.

🔗 The "Correlation Constraint" — Why It Matters

Standard matrix factorization (SVD) decomposes the user-item interaction matrix to find latent factors, but treats each behavioral signal independently. The correlation constraint adds a crucial regularization:

By projecting item factors through the eigenvectors of the behavioral correlation matrix, we ensure that latent representations encode known relationships between user behaviors. For example, in our data:

  • add_to_cart and purchase have r = 0.569 — items attractive to "cart-adders" should also appeal to "purchasers"
  • click_count and session_duration are moderately correlated — engaged clickers tend to have longer sessions
  • scroll_depth and purchase are negatively correlated (r = -0.085) — deep scrollers may be "browsing" rather than buying

Without the correlation constraint, the model might learn item factors that ignore these behavioral relationships. With it, recommendations are more behaviorally coherent.

  • Frontend: Vanilla HTML/CSS/JavaScript with Chart.js
